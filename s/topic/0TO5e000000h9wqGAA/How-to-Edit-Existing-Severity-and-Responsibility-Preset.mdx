---
title: "How to Edit Existing Severity and Responsibility Preset"
description: " If your handling preferences for certain issues change over time, you can easily update your existing presets. This guide walks you through how to add, edit, and delete severity and responsibility presets to reflect your current workflows."
---

From your Latchel Dashboard, click the **initials of your account** located at the top right corner. And on the dropdown, select **Account Settings**.

<Frame>
  ![1latchel Pn](/images/1Latchel.png)
</Frame>

Then on the **Severity/Responsibility** tab, click the ellipses to show **Edit Presets**.

<Frame>
  ![Image 05 16 2025 12 57 AM Pn](/images/image-05-16-2025_12_57_AM.png)
</Frame>

On the Severity and Responsibility Presets page, you can:

- Add a new preset
- Add new settings on preset
- Edit existing presets
- Delete existing presets

We’ll walk through each of these options in the sections below.

### **Add New Preset**

Follow steps above in order to access the Severity and Responsibility Presets page. Then, click **\+New Set**.

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 10 40 AM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_10_40_AM.png)
</Frame>

**Enter the name** of the new preset and **provide a description** for the settings set. Click **Create** to save.

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 10 42 AM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_10_42_AM.png)
</Frame>

Your new preset will now appear in the list of available presets. You can now edit it to add responsibility settings and their corresponding severity controls.

### **Add New Settings on Presets**

Click the **name of the preset** to which you want to add new settings.

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 10 50 AM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_10_50_AM.png)
</Frame>

Then, click **\+New Setting**.

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 10 51 AM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_10_51_AM.png)
</Frame>

You will be prompted to choose a problem & location combination. Start by clicking **Choose Problem**.

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 10 52 AM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_10_52_AM.png)
</Frame>

**Choose from the list of Problem Categories** by expanding the desired category. Locate the problem within the selected category, then click **Choose**.

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 11 17 AM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_11_17_AM.png)
</Frame>

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 11 18 AM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_11_18_AM.png)
</Frame>

Next, configure the location settings by clicking, **Choose Location**.

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 11 56 AM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_11_56_AM.png)
</Frame>

Expand the list of location settings, then click **Choose** next to your selected location.

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 11 59 AM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_11_59_AM.png)
</Frame>

Next, choose how jobs created with this Problem/Location combination will proceed:

- **Approval Required** – Jobs under this setting will require your approval before processing.
- **Resident Responsible** – Residents will be responsible for the costs of any future jobs under this setting.
- **Control Severity** – Select the appropriate severity level:
  1. Normal
  2. Urgent
  3. Emergency

Click **Save** once you've finished setting the problem priority.

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 12 09 PM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_12_09_PM.png)
</Frame>

The new setting will now be added to the preset.

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 12 12 PM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_12_12_PM.png)
</Frame>

### **Delete Existing Preset**

If a severity and responsibility preset is no longer needed, you can easily remove it to keep your settings organized and up to date.

To delete a preset, click the **hyperlinked name of the preset**.

<Frame>
  ![Latchel 05 16 2025 10 50 AM Pn](/images/Latchel-05-16-2025_10_50_AM.png)
</Frame>

 Click the **ellipses** at the top right, then finally click **Delete**.

<Frame>
  ![Image 05 22 2025 09 30 AM Pn](/images/image-05-22-2025_09_30_AM.png)
</Frame>

<Warning>
  Deleting a preset will remove all custom handling instructions tied to that issue type. Any future work orders that would have matched this preset will follow your fallback settings instead.
</Warning>
